Introduction:

Back in 2018, one animated movie by the name of "Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse" was released in theaters. The film was very well recieved between critics and viewers, for it's unique artstyle and experimental animation. The artstyle and animation was made to feel like you were watching a live action comic book, which the film captures perfectly. For example, they would make the framerate lower for certain characters in the film during moments where they want the characters movement appear swift by limiting the amount of frames you see for the character. With myself being a huge fan of this movie, I wanted to do a "year in review" of how this animation style has been the inspiration of animation movies in the last year.
