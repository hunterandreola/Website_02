The Animation

Similar to "Into The Spider-Verse," this film takes inspiration during its action sequences. You can tell by abserving the quick movements, either when the wolf runs or swings his sickles, that the animation seems to almost skip frames. This gives us more unique ways to show the speed and power of this wolf, making these movements super quick and snappy.

The Artstyle

Instead of going for a more realistic animation, like the previous Puss in Boots film, this film goes for a more artistic artstyle that resembles similarities to oil paintings and makes you feel like you are watching a fairytale. Looking closely, you can see brush strokes on the hat and cape, that enforce the artstyle.
